Lifestyle Habits

  • Smoking: I do not smoke. I do smoke non-nicotine shisha, but stopped nicotine shisha when launching shisha bars as it worsened my atopic dermatitis.
  • Alcohol: I do not drink. I used to drink when managing bars, but currently, alcohol allergy and atopic dermatitis worsen, making me itch after just one beer, so I stopped.
  • All-nighters (playing out all night): I do not pull all-nighters. I used to enjoy them, but recently, I prioritize my morning routine, and disrupting it makes my day unstable and disorganized due to my weak willpower. While Tokyo has many places to play until morning, in Kyrgyzstan and Kazakhstan, there are only clubs, bars, and 24-hour cafes, and friends who would join me are rare, so it faded from my habits. I'm usually home by 9:00 PM.

Relationship with the Internet

The concept of "slow internet" has been proposed, but I often feel tired and "don't want to do anything anymore" due to the overwhelming amount of information I receive daily, exceeding my information management and collection capacity. Also, even after summarizing and abstracting content, I sometimes can't recall details from movies or books when asked by friends. Therefore, I try to write down my impressions and critiques of web articles, books, and movies whenever I notice. This not only helps me remember good articles I've read but also allows me to understand what I felt, realized, and learned from them, serving as good practice for "thinking for myself."

Daily Routine (Including Ideal)

I wake up at 5 AM, brush my teeth, wash my face, style my hair, shave, do skincare, and drink water. Then, while listening to music, I grind coffee beans. I meditate for 5 minutes, visualize for 10 minutes, do affirmations for 1 minute, and journal for 10 minutes. After that, I stretch for 20 minutes, then study languages for 30 minutes. If my shoulders get tired, I take a walk or stretch and read 10 pages of a book. Afterward, I make a light breakfast and start work. Work involves doing something beneficial for others. I start at 7:00 AM and continue until 10:00 AM. After that, it varies by day, but most of my decision-making requiring mental effort is completed during the morning. I then go outside to relax. In the evening, I return home by 9:00 PM and turn off the lights by 10:00 PM. On nights when I can't sleep, I allow myself to stay awake but always make sure to wake up by 5 AM.
